FIRST BOOK 2020 IMPACT REPORT

From Our CEO

Kyle Zimmer
President, CEO, Co-Founder
Together, we will ensure that the needs of millions of children growing up in poverty are not an afterthought, but a significant force for educational equity.
Dear Friends:

It has become a cliché to describe the challenges of 2020 – so I will simply say this: The needs have never been greater and the opportunity for real change has never been more hopeful.

First Book started the year by bringing together policy, non-profit, and executive leaders from our corporate partners for our first-ever Co-Authoring Impact Summit. The January Summit showcased how these partnerships have accelerated educational opportunities for millions of children, and engaged leaders in discussions of innovative ways to build momentum.

Just two months later, life was drastically changed by the global pandemic – making our work together even more critical.

The COVID-19 pandemic not only exacerbated the educational crisis for children in poverty, it also caused enormous hardship for millions more families. In addition, the issues of racism and inequality came to the forefront again after the killing of George Floyd and others.

In the pages ahead you’ll read how First Book’s integrated models – amplifying the collective voice of more than half a million educators in the First Book Network – and cross-sector partnerships enabled powerful, educator-driven responses to these challenges.

You’ll also hear from the real heroes: our nation’s educators. Educators are on a high-wire act, juggling remote and hybrid learning, delivering books to students’ front doors, and going above and beyond. Educators need our help like never before.

As we look ahead to 2021, it’s clear that the effects of COVID-19 will impact our children and families long after the vaccines are distributed.

First Book’s systemic approach, driven by educators on the frontlines, provides a powerful and proven combination to address educational inequities. By actively engaging educators throughout our process, we are piloting, testing, refining, and scaling innovative resources and new collaborations to support educational equity and to further the sector for anyone working in the lives of children in poverty.

We thank each and every one of you for your support.

With gratitude,

Kyle

But for every student in need, First Book, its network of educators, and amazing partners have been working day and night to support, provide resources, and ensure that no child falls behind.

COVID-19 Response Snapshot

Covid 19 Response Survey


With our partner Intel Corporation®, we conducted a nationwide COVID-19 Response Survey to identify our educators’ key areas of concern regarding how the pandemic was impacting their students and the resources needed to support them.

2,600+ educators reported these challenges:
  • Mental/emotional health of kids and families — 87% were more concerned with their students facing mental/emotional health challenges than prior to the pandemic.
  • Access to learning tools and resources — 86% were more concerned about students having no books at home than prior to the pandemic.
  • A widening digital divide — 40% of their students didn’t have access to reliable internet at home and 37% did not have access to functioning digital devices.
  • An extended “summer slide” — 78% of educators were especially concerned about how the extended learning gap would affect the students they serve.

Creating Learning Connections Initiative


With CDW-G, Intel Corporation®, and the Lego Foundation, we provided a new turnkey First Book grants model, which enabled selected school districts to outline their needs for resources to address the digital divide and enable remote learning for more students.

  • 46 school districts received Creating Learning Connections grants
  • 17states impacted with new devices and connectivity awards
  • 1,500 new educators received access to First Book resources
  • 14,554 devices and digital learning resources from Intel and CDW-G, valued at more than $5 million
  • $4,000 to each awarded school district to support home internet connectivity
  • 7500 LEGO Education solutions, valued at $2 million,from the LegoLEGO Foundation to support STEAM learning at home and in school
